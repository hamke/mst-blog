1. Identity Verification Issues During Sign-Up

In China, many services require identity verification via SMS for account registration.

Common Situations Requiring SMS Verification in China

  • Signing up for major Chinese online services (portals, games, etc.)
  • Opening a Chinese bank account or using financial services
  • Using payment apps like WeChat Pay or Alipay
  • Booking flights and trains within China
  • Using shared bikes in China

However, if you don’t have a local Chinese phone number or you live abroad (e.g., in Korea), the SMS verification process can be a hurdle.

In such cases, using a virtual Chinese phone number allows you to receive SMS messages easily via the internet without needing a physical SIM card.

2. Protecting Personal Information

Entering your Korean phone number indiscriminately when registering on Chinese websites may pose privacy risks.

Due to the relatively weak cybersecurity environment in China, your personal information might be unintentionally exposed.

By using a virtual phone number service solely for receiving SMS, you can avoid entering your real number and better protect your sensitive information.
